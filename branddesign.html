Silent Film Fest Brand Design

For this project, I designed a visual identity for a silent film festival in our branding class, inspired by the expressive language of early cinema. I used punctuation as a graphic device to echo the emotions and gestures crucial to silent films. Starting with a focus on emotion and typography, I explored scale, spacing, and placement to convey meaning without words. The final concept offers a modern take on 1920s storytelling, which subtly evokes silence through form rather than stating it outright.

This project began with an exploration of ideas. Most rely on integrating contemporary with the audible silence of silent movies. Out of all the studies, the use of punctuation to represent silence was the chosen concept.
